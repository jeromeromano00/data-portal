Commuting-Zone Measures of Style, Norms, and Cultural Change

This portal provides commuting-zone-year aggregates constructed for Image(s), by Hans-Joachim Voth and David Yanagizawa-Drott. The underlying project uses more than 55 million U.S. high school yearbook portraits to study cultural change, the diffusion of new styles, convergence in male and female appearance, and spatial polarization in social norms from 1930 to 2010.

The downloadable series below are intended as a transparent replication and access point for the paper's commuting-zone measures.

About This Dataset

The data below summarize annual measures of style and norm deviation at the commuting-zone level. Use the selector panel to preview a series or download a filtered CSV for a specific commuting zone and period.

Dataset scope

The portal reports annual commuting-zone aggregates derived from U.S. high school senior portraits between 1930 and 2010. These measures are designed to capture local cultural change and stylistic divergence over time.

Style Novelty

Style Novelty captures the prevalence of style combinations that were observed among fewer than 1 percent of students up to and including a given year. It is intended to measure the arrival of genuinely new stylistic combinations.

Horizontal Norm Deviation

Horizontal Norm Deviation measures how far a student's style differs from that of contemporaneous peers within the same high school. Higher values indicate weaker conformity to the local peer environment.

Vertical Norm Deviation

Vertical Norm Deviation measures how different a cohort's style is from that of students in the same high school twenty years earlier. Higher values indicate greater intergenerational stylistic change.

Gender-specific variants are reported with the suffixes _m for men and _f for women. The raw index is labeled Norm Deviation Raw Index.
